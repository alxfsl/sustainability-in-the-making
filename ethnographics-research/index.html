EthnoGRAPHICS: doing design research one drawing at a time

Alessandra Fasoli October 4, 2023

EthnoGRAPHIC is the methodology underlying Sustainaibility in the Making. It’s a visual ethnographic approach that uses graphic language both as a medium of inquiry (gathering and annotating data), as a medium of data analysis (making sense of data), and as a medium of data visualisation (data storytelling). Having worked as a graphic designer and being myself a urban sketcher , it is probably no surprise that I decided to rely on drawing as a my primary research tool. However, EthnoGRAPHIC is a multi-faceted methodology I am developing for my research that comprises various tools, this website being one of them.

As I am still working on my research, this website is a work-in-progress to that I am using to both showcase the work, reflect upon it, and finally tell the stories I am collecting.

Alessandra Fasoli

I'm a design researcher and a PhD candidate at Kingston School of Art, London. Sustainability in the Making is my craft-based PhD where I am trying to learn how craft contributes to generate cultures of sustainability, through my craft: drawing and web design. I am the designer and researcher behind this website, which is a part of my PhD research. 

I also work as a lecturer and a freelance designer, occasionally taking illustration commissions. 

My main areas of research are design anthropology, sustainable design, design for social innovation, craft and material cultures, craft and design activism.

I draw. A lot
