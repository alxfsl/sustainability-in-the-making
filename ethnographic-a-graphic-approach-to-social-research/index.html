EthnoGRAPHIC: A Graphic Approach to Social Research 

Alessandra Fasoli October 4, 2023

EthnoGRAPHIC is the methodology underlying Sustainaibility in the Making. It might be described as putting back ‘graphic’ in ethnography. In simpler words, it’s a visual ethnographic approach that uses graphic language both as a medium of inquiry (gathering and annotating data), as a medium of data analysis (making sense of data), and as a medium of data visualisation (data storytelling). Having worked as a graphic designer and being myself a urban sketcher , it is probably no surprise that I decided to rely on drawing as a my primary research tool. However, EthnoGRAPHIC is a multi-faceted methodology I am developing for my research that comprises various tools, this website being one of them.

As I am still working on my research, this website is a work-in-progress to that I am using to both showcase the work, reflect upon it, and finally tell the stories I am collecting.

Alessandra Fasoli

I'm a design researcher and a PhD candidate at Kingston School of Art, London. Sustainability in the Making is my craft-based PhD where I am trying to grasp how cultures of sustainability are generated through collective craft, through my craft: drawing and web design. I am the designer and researcher behind this website, which is meant a part of the PhD research process. 

I also work as a lecturer and a freelance designer, occasionally taking illustration commissions. 

My main areas of research are design anthropology, sustainability, critical theory, and environmental sociology.
