Sustainability in the Making

Craft Programmes for Eco-Social Change

A craft-based PhD that explores how cultures of making can contribute to sustainability change.

¬†

Sustainability in the Making ¬†(formerly: Craft in Makerspaces. The Potential for Social Change for Sustainability) is a practice-based doctoral training partnership sponsored by AHRC Techne.

Based at Kingston School of Art in partnership with the Crafts Council, this research started as an investigation of the cultural role of makerspaces. However, following considerations emerging from social and environmental changes over the last few years, I decided to take a broader social and cultural perspective on making which goes a bit beyond makerspaces.


This is an ethnoGRAPHIC journey through craft, drawings, and maker stories.

The Art of Crafting Sustainability

An ethno/GRAPHIC study through observational sketching

The Art of Crafting Sustainability is a visual and artistic ethnoGRAPHIC project that uses observational sketching and drawing to investigate how makers in the Southwest are using their practice to promote and build sustainability behaviours through programmes and workshops.
